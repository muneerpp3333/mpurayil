## FAQ

### What is CQRS & Event Sourcing and why does it matter?

CQRS & Event Sourcing is a critical architectural pattern for modern software systems. It matters because it directly impacts scalability, maintainability, and team velocity in production environments.

### How does Go compare for CQRS & Event Sourcing?

Go offers specific advantages for CQRS & Event Sourcing including strong typing, ecosystem support, and production-grade tooling. The choice depends on your team's expertise and project requirements.

### What are common mistakes with CQRS & Event Sourcing?

Common mistakes include premature optimization, insufficient observability, ignoring failure modes, and over-engineering the initial implementation. Start simple and iterate based on production data.

### How long does it take to implement CQRS & Event Sourcing?

Implementation timelines vary significantly based on scale and complexity. A minimal viable implementation typically takes 2-4 weeks, while a production-grade system may require 2-3 months of iterative development.

### What infrastructure do I need for CQRS & Event Sourcing?

Infrastructure requirements depend on scale. At minimum, you need reliable compute, monitoring, and CI/CD. For production systems, add load balancing, auto-scaling, and comprehensive observability.
