## FAQ

### What is Distributed Caching and why does it matter?

Distributed Caching is a critical architectural pattern for modern software systems. It matters because it directly impacts scalability, maintainability, and team velocity in production environments.

### How does Rust compare for Distributed Caching?

Rust offers specific advantages for Distributed Caching including strong typing, ecosystem support, and production-grade tooling. The choice depends on your team's expertise and project requirements.

### What are common mistakes with Distributed Caching?

Common mistakes include premature optimization, insufficient observability, ignoring failure modes, and over-engineering the initial implementation. Start simple and iterate based on production data.

### How long does it take to implement Distributed Caching?

Implementation timelines vary significantly based on scale and complexity. A minimal viable implementation typically takes 2-4 weeks, while a production-grade system may require 2-3 months of iterative development.

### What infrastructure do I need for Distributed Caching?

Infrastructure requirements depend on scale. At minimum, you need reliable compute, monitoring, and CI/CD. For production systems, add load balancing, auto-scaling, and comprehensive observability.
